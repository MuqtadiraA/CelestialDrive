1967 Chevrolet Corvette Stingray Convertible L71

Starting at $4,438 (1967), Current Value: $175,000 - $400,000+

The 1967 Corvette Stingray Convertible L71 is the epitome of the C2 Corvette generation. Known for its striking curves, bold performance, and unmistakable muscle car presence, the '67 Stingray is a legendary icon. The L71 variant, with its 427 cubic-inch V8 and Tri-Power carburetors, remains one of the most sought-after classic Corvettes ever produced.

Performance & Powertrain

Under the hood, the L71 variant packs a 427 cubic-inch V8 engine with Tri-Power carburetors, delivering:

  • 435 hp @ 5800 rpm
  • 460 lb-ft torque @ 4000 rpm
  • 0–60 mph in approximately 4.8 seconds
  • Top Speed: 140 mph

Paired with a 4-speed manual Muncie gearbox, the driving experience is pure muscle car adrenaline.

Exterior Design

The '67 Stingray's design is a testament to classic American muscle with its sleek, aggressive styling:

  • Body Style: 2-door convertible
  • Unique "stinger" hood scoop with contrasting paint
  • Refined side vents and sleeker fender flares
  • Optional bolt-on aluminum wheels
  • Flip-up concealed headlights for a clean front-end design
  • Convertible top options: Vinyl or optional black/dark blue canvas

Interior

Inside, the Corvette Stingray delivers a vintage yet luxurious experience:

  • Optional leather-trimmed seats in black, red, saddle, or teal
  • Classic dual-cowl dashboard with center-mounted analog clock
  • Optional teakwood steering wheel
  • AM/FM push-button radio for classic tunes
  • Dash-mounted tachometer and optional factory air conditioning

Pricing & Market Value

Originally priced at ~$4,438 USD in 1967, the Corvette Stingray Convertible L71 now commands a premium in the market:

  • Good Condition: $175,000 – $200,000
  • Fully Restored / Showroom Condition: $250,000 – $350,000+
  • Matching Numbers / Rare Options (e.g., side pipes, factory hardtop): $400,000+
